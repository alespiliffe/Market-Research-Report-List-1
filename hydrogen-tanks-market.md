<p><p>Hydrogen tanks are categorized into five types based on their construction materials and pressure levels. </p><p>Type 1 tanks are all-metal, cylindrical, and capable of storing hydrogen at high pressure. Type 2 tanks are metallic but feature a composite overwrap for added strength and reduced weight. Type 3 tanks are primarily composite with a metal liner, offering lightweight and high-pressure storage. Type 4 tanks are fully composite with a polymer liner, enhancing safety and reducing weight. Type 5, a newer category, focuses on advanced materials for optimized performance and durability.</p></p>
