<p><p>The twelve-string mandolin market encompasses various types based on size and tuning, including soprano, alto, tenor, bass, and contrabass. Soprano mandolins are small and bright-sounding, ideal for melody lines. Alto mandolins produce a warmer tone and are slightly larger. Tenor mandolins offer deeper resonance, making them suited for harmonic support. Bass mandolins provide a rich, low-end sound, while contrabass mandolins deliver even deeper tones for a fuller orchestral range. Each type serves unique musical roles in ensembles.</p></p>
